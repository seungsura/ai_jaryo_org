# Translationese Checklist

Use this as a review checklist, not a blind replacement table. A phrase can be acceptable when the technical context needs it.

## Core Test

Ask three questions:

1. Would a Korean developer naturally say this when explaining the idea out loud?
2. Is the sentence built around a living verb, or around stacked nouns?
3. Does the sentence preserve the source claim without becoming softer or more bureaucratic?

If the answer is no, rewrite.

## Common Patterns

| Warning sign | Why it feels awkward | Better move |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `AI에 의해 생성된 코드` | English passive order sits inside Korean. | `AI가 만든 코드` |
| `검증이 수행된다` | Actor disappears and the verb becomes stiff. | `검증한다`, `검증이 돈다`, `검증을 붙인다` |
| `~의 측면에서` | Abstract frame often hides the real claim. | `~으로 보면`, or remove the frame and state the claim. |
| `~라고 볼 수 있다` | Weak report ending. | Say the claim directly when evidence supports it. |
| `핵심은 ~ 데 있다` | Methodology-translation rhythm. | `중요한 것은 ~이다`, or stronger: `결국 ~해야 한다`. |
| `~을 통해서` overuse | A connector replaces the real verb. | Use the real action verb: `검증으로 막는다`, `파일에 남긴다`. |
| `A의 B의 C` | Heavy noun chain. | Break into a clause or reorder around a verb. |
| `상류/하류` as a vague metaphor | Imported process metaphor can sound stiff. | Use `앞단`, `뒤쪽`, `먼저`, `나중에` when that is the real meaning. |
| `강하게 호출된다` | English-like abstraction. | `자주 필요해진다`, `여기서 쓰인다`, `여기서 중요해진다`. |
| `장표` | Meeting/consulting jargon; user rejected it. | `슬라이드`, `발표 자료`. |

## Sentence Moves

- Put the important actor near the verb.
- Move modifiers close to the word they modify.
- Keep one sentence to one main claim unless the contrast needs to stay together.
- Change noun piles into verb clauses.
- Delete throat-clearing phrases before polishing style.
- Keep repeated key terms when repetition helps the argument; do not replace them with vague pronouns for variety.

## Seminar Prose Moves

- Treat prose as something the presenter can say aloud. If a sentence only works on paper, rewrite it.
- Spoken Korean here does not mean casual filler. Remove `그러니까`, `뭔가`, `사실은` when they only loosen the sentence.
- Prefer `문제는 X가 아니라 Y입니다` when the chapter turns on contrast.
- Prefer short assertive endings after a long explanatory sentence.
- Do not write every paragraph with the same length and rhythm.
- Do not summarize too early. Let the paragraph push the reader from problem to consequence to answer.

## Slide Copy Moves

- Keep the slide-copy rules even when prose becomes more spoken. The slide should not become a transcript.
- Slide copy can be shorter than prose, but it must not become a slogan with lost meaning.
- Numeric and evidence slides may use `metric card trio`, `evidence cards without tables`, or `evidence table`; do not force them into one-line statement slides.
- A slide title should name the scene or claim, not explain the whole paragraph.

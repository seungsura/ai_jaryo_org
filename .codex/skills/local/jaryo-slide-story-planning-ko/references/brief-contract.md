# Slide Brief Contract

Use this shape for every planned slide.

- `slide id`
- `source section`
- `slide purpose`
- `one-sentence claim`
- `supporting points`
- `must-keep terms`
- `do-not-say`
- `recommended layout family`
- `reference shell`
- `evidence requirement`
- `speaker intent`

## Brief Quality Rules

- `slide purpose` and `claim` must not repeat each other word-for-word.
- `supporting points` should usually stay between 2 and 4.
- `must-keep terms` are reserved for exact English terms or terminology that should not be softened away in later copy stages.
- `do-not-say` should capture risky framing, overclaim, or wording that creates a slide-tone violation.
- `reference shell` should come from `docs/03-html/shared/layout-shell-reference.md` and stay fixed through HTML build unless the outline is re-approved.
- `evidence requirement` should state whether the slide needs proof, citation, example, or no explicit evidence.

## What Approved Means

Only approved fields should be promoted into `slide-outline.md` or `manifest.md`. Working notes, alternatives, and reviewer debate stay in internal agent output.
